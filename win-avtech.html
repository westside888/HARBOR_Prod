The Problem

The Skills Are
There.The Path Isn't.

Highly skilled military aviation professionals leave service every year without FAA certification — not because they lack experience, but because the process is complex, time-sensitive, and difficult to navigate alone.

Without the right support, many never complete certification and are unable to enter the civilian aviation workforce — despite years of hands-on experience maintaining some of the most advanced aircraft in the world.

"The gap isn't ability — it's access. Most programs stop at training. This one doesn't."
The Solution

A Complete Path from
Service to Certification.

What AvTech

How It
Works.

Four Steps · Start to Finish
01
Warriors In Need
Intake & Eligibility Review

Service members complete a short intake form. WIN's team reviews military experience and determines the appropriate FAA certification pathway.

02

03

Candidates complete all FAA testing — General, Airframe, and Powerplant — and obtain their A&P certification through AvTech's coordinated examiner network.
